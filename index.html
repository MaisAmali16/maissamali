StudyBuddy

UX · Gamified habit-building

StudyBuddy is a Tomodachi-style mobile app that helps students build better study habits by linking academic activity to the wellbeing of a virtual pet. When users log study sessions, their pet gains energy and levels up—turning effort into visible progress. Goals, gentle reminders, streaks, and rewards are designed to keep momentum without feeling strict or overwhelming. Pets evolve based on the subjects a user studies, making each one feel personal while still grounded in real study routines.

CoinLand

Hackathon · Financial literacy game

CoinLand is an educational stock-market simulation game designed to help children (ages 7–12) build financial literacy and confidence with money. The experience simplifies complex ideas such as budgeting, investing, and market behavior into approachable, interactive systems grounded in real-world companies. Players make decisions, track progress, and manage resources within a controlled market environment shaped by in-game news and player activity. Game elements like customization, rewards, and a virtual space are used intentionally to motivate learning and reinforce positive financial habits, creating a safe and engaging introduction to real-world money concepts.

Pulse Canvas

Interactive · Generative visuals

Pulse Canvas is an interactive experience where technology transforms emotional signals into visual art during a reflective two-person conversation. Each session produces a unique generative artwork shaped by rhythm, intensity, and interaction. By responding to heart rate, vocal dynamics, and facial cues, the system visualizes interpersonal signals as abstract forms, allowing participants to sense the other person’s presence in a nonverbal, shared way. The goal is not to diagnose emotion, but to create a calmer space for listening, empathy, and awareness.

NeighborGoods

Web · Community lending

NeighborGoods is a community lending website built around a practical alternative to overconsumption: borrow what you need instead of constantly buying new. The project makes the “library economy” feel approachable by combining clear structure, friendly tone, and easy navigation. It connects individual convenience (saving money and accessing items) with a broader outcome (reducing waste), presenting sustainability as something usable in everyday life rather than a distant ideal.

Dino Voyage

Max/MSP · Audio-visual story

Dino Voyage is an audio-visual narrative created in Max/MSP, centered on “The Quest for the Ultimate Plant-based Planet.” Max was chosen because it lets sound and visuals behave like one system: rhythm, timing, and texture can shape motion and atmosphere in real time. The piece is built to feel cohesive and playful, where audio cues and visual changes reinforce each other so the story lands through synchronized moments, pacing, and mood.
